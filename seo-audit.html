Part 1 — Critical Issues (Fix Before Marketing)

1Contact Page Has No Real Contact Info

Every field shows dashes — no phone, no email, no address, no hours. Kills trust on first visit.

Fix Add a real NSN email, a Google Voice business number, hours (Mon-Fri 9AM-6PM CT), and a HQ city (Nashville, TN is fine).

2Featured Vendors Show 0 Reviews

Homepage vendor cards display "★★★★★ (0)" — looks fake and untrustworthy to any serious buyer.

Fix Hide star display until a vendor has at least 1 review. Replace with "New Member" badge.

3Crime Analytics Listed as Key Feature — Not Live

It's in the nav, on the homepage, on the How It Works page — all "coming soon." Clients click it and hit a dead end.

Fix Remove from primary navigation. Add a "Coming Q3 2026 — Join Waitlist" page so it builds a list instead of disappointing visitors.

4Job Board Features in Main Nav Confuse Buyers

"Post Resume" and "Search Resumes" are visible in the primary nav. Clients who want to hire a security company see job board links and get confused about what NSN even does.

Fix Move to a "For Professionals" dropdown. Main nav should be buyer/vendor focused only.

5Page Titles Are Duplicated

"How It Works | Nationwide Security Network | Nationwide Security Network" — doubles the brand name. Bad for SEO.

Fix Fix meta title template — should be "Page Name | Nationwide Security Network"

6Search Bar Placeholder Text is Truncated

Shows "Search vendors, privat..." — gets cut off. Looks sloppy.

Fix Change to "Search by location, service type, or vendor name..."

7Pricing is Too Low — Signals Cheap Directory

$55-$100/mo is well below market. Security companies pay $149-$299/mo on comparable platforms. Low price = low perceived quality.

Fix See Part 4 — Pricing Update.

8Zero Social Proof Anywhere on Site

No logos, no testimonials, no client counts, no "trusted by" section. Every serious B2B platform has this.

Fix Add stats bar on homepage: "1,000+ Vendors Listed | 50 States Covered | Free for Clients" and add 2-3 vendor testimonials.

9Footer is Bare — Missing Key Links

No social links, no blog, no state pages, no service category pages, no resources section.

Fix Expand footer to include: Resources, Browse by State, Browse by Service, LinkedIn, Blog (even if empty now).

10"Smart Matching" Nav Link Goes Nowhere Useful

It's a feature, not a page destination. Clicking it leaves visitors confused.

Fix Remove from main nav. Fold it into How It Works and the About page as a described feature.

Part 5 — SEO Landing Page Strategy

State landing pages are the #1 long-term organic moat. "Security companies in [state]" searches get 8,000-45,000 searches/month depending on the state. This is free traffic that converts directly into client signups. Hermes writes all the content — developer just needs the URL structure in place.

50 State Pages

/security-companies/texas
/security-companies/california
/security-companies/new-york
/security-companies/florida

Pre-filtered vendor search for each state. Includes state-specific intro, service categories, featured vendors, RFP CTA, and FAQ block.

10 Service Pages

/services/armed-security-guards
/services/mobile-patrol
/services/executive-protection
/services/event-security

Service-specific pages with filtered vendor results, what to look for, how to hire, and average costs.

50 City Pages (Phase 2)

/security-companies/texas/houston
/security-companies/texas/dallas
/security-companies/california/los-angeles

Hyper-local pages targeting "security guard companies in Houston" type searches. Build after state pages rank.

Service+State Combos (Phase 3)

/services/armed-guards/texas
/services/executive-protection/california
/services/event-security/florida

High-intent combo pages. Someone searching "armed guards Texas" is ready to buy. Build top 50 combos.

State Page Template Structure

Each state page includes:

1. H1: "Security Companies in [State] | NSN"

2. State-specific 3-sentence intro paragraph (mentions key industries, cities, market context)

3. Pre-filtered vendor search widget for that state

4. Stats bar: "[X] Vendors in [State] | 15 Service Types | Free for Clients"

5. Top 6 service categories with icons (tailored to state)

6. 3 featured vendors from that state (dynamically pulled)

7. "Post a Free RFP to [State] Vendors" CTA section

8. 5-question FAQ block (state-specific SEO — licensing, costs, common services)

9. Neighboring states sidebar links (cross-links for SEO juice)
